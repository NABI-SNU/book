# Introduction

Welcome to the NABI seminar book.

This seminar series is designed as an introductory pathway for new NABI members. Its goal is to introduce the core ideas of **computational neuroscience** and **NeuroAI** through a structured sequence of concept-focused lectures and hands-on tutorials.

The material in this book is adapted from Neuromatch Academy Computational Neuroscience and NeuroAI courses and reorganized for NABI's internal onboarding seminar format.

## Seminar Format

Each session is designed to fit within roughly 90 minutes. A session may include:

1. practice exercises for preparation or review
2. a concept-centered lecture of about 40 minutes
3. a hands-on tutorial of about 40 minutes
4. discussion of research applications

The lecture and tutorial components are the core required pieces of each session.

## Curriculum Overview

The overall curriculum is organized as a progressive introduction to computational approaches for studying brains and intelligent systems.

It begins with **foundational machine learning tools** for fitting models to data, interpreting representations, and building intuition about how models can be used in neuroscience. From there, it expands into **probabilistic inference**, where hidden variables, uncertainty, and decision-making become central.

The curriculum then moves toward **dynamical systems** and latent-state models, which provide a language for thinking about temporal structure, internal dynamics, and evolving neural processes. In the broader seminar trajectory, these ideas extend naturally toward **reinforcement learning** and **geometry**, where questions of action, representation, and structure become increasingly important.

The current curated book follows that progression through a session-based structure:

- **Session_1** introduces model fitting, dimensionality reduction, and deep learning.
- **Session_2** focuses on Bayesian inference and probabilistic reasoning.
- **Session_3** introduces hidden dynamics through hidden Markov models.
- **Session_6** explores representation geometry by comparing artificial and biological neural networks.

Together, these sessions are meant to give new members a common conceptual starting point and a shared practical foundation for later reading groups, projects, and research discussions within NABI.
